Computational Research Facility

High Performance Computing (HPC) at LNMIIT

HPC is a very useful tool in many areas of Science and Engineering Research. There are a large number of researchers across disciplines involved in HPC at LNMIIT Jaipur. The LNMIIT recently acquired a High Performance Computing machine from HP — HPC2014.

140 Cores 672 GB Memory 7 Nodes Intel Xeon Ivy Bridge 2.8 GHz QDR InfiniBand

⚙ Hardware

  • 7 nodes — 6 compute nodes + 1 master node
  • Powered by HP (Hewlett-Packard) with 2 CPUs per node
  • Intel Xeon E5-2680V2 (Ivy Bridge), 10 cores per processor
  • Total: 140 computing cores across 7 nodes
  • 96 GB RAM per node — 672 GB total memory
  • HP ProLiant SL230s Gen8 server platform

💻 Software

  • Operating System: RHEL (Red Hat Enterprise Linux)
  • Intel Cluster Studio XE 2013 — comprehensive HPC suite
  • Intel FORTRAN, C, C++ compilers & libraries
  • Industry-leading MPI library for parallel computing
  • Integrated tool suite for HPC development & debugging
  • High-speed QDR InfiniBand network for ultra-fast data transfer

// About Us

Our Laboratory

Established in 2003 the undergraduate laboratory for 1st year B.Tech students is equipped with several experiments in different branches in Physics. The lab is divided into two sections, one is UG Physics Lab and the other is UG Physics Optics Lab. Laboratory work is a very important part of a course in General Physics. It reinforces the student's understanding of fundamental concepts and principles while, at the same time, helping the student to develop skill in carrying out scientific measurements. It is designed in such a way that the students can understand the basics of Physics through hands on experiments.
